02 / WHAT IS THIS?

This single-page layout is a playground for bold UI decisions: thick borders, layered shadows, flat colors that collide instead of blending. It’s designed to make every scroll feel like flicking through oversized zines in a digital arcade.

You can repurpose this as a personal site, portfolio, landing page or even a design manifesto. All components are pure HTML, Tailwind and a tiny sprinkle of JavaScript for interaction.

Design Rules

  • Always visible structure (borders everywhere).
  • Typography is loud and slightly misaligned.
  • Shadows look more like stickers than realism.
  • Colors clash on purpose, not by accident.

Tech Stack

Tailwind CSS via CDN, vanilla JavaScript for scroll monitoring + intersection observers, and semantic HTML.

03 / INTERACTION MODULES

Each module below is an independent UI block that reacts to scroll, hover or layout changes. Mix, match, rip apart.

Scroll-reactive HUD

module: 01

A floating “status panel” at the top of the page monitors your viewport width, scroll depth and mood color. The values update as you move, turning the layout into a living dashboard.

Brutal scroll reveals

module: 02

Sections and cards slide up into place using intersection observers. No heavy animation libraries — just timing and tension.

Layout that misbehaves

module: 03

On small screens, everything stacks like chunky blocks. On larger displays, the page turns into a split-screen dashboard with a live side panel.

Responsive without being shy

module: 04

Instead of hiding elements on mobile, we just compress and re-stack them, preserving the anarchic energy of the desktop layout.

04 / SCROLL LAB

This strip of panels is built as a mini storyboard. Scroll slowly — the cards will pop into view and form a loose narrative about motion.

Hint: Try flicking the scroll wheel vs. using trackpad gestures. The experience feels different because the animations are tuned for both micro and macro movements.

Panel 01 [enter]

Scroll enters. The first block snaps in. You realise the layout is aware of you.

Panel 02 [drift]

As you drift downward, cards align in a grid that barely holds itself together.

Panel 03 [impact]

Bright elements collide with dark ones. The composition becomes more intentional.

Panel 04 [loop]

Repeating patterns emerge. Your eye loops across the layout in wide diagonals.

Panel 05 [pause]

Negative space returns. You’re invited to breathe before the final flick.

Panel 06 [exit]

You hit the bottom and snap back to the top. Neo-brutalism loves loops.
